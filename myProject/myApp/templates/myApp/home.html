ABOUT ME

Myroslava Grygorachyk

The Woman I Used to Be

I grew up believing a "good woman" puts others first, that if I worked hard, stayed strong, and didn't complain, I was worthy of love. Even after leaving an abusive relationship and starting over in Canada with my little boy, I found myself repeating old patterns: overgiving, criticizing myself, and feeling like I was never enough. I was raising three boys, working full-time, and always showing up as "the strong one." But inside, I felt overwhelmed, exhausted, unseen, and deeply disconnected from who I really was.

The Turning Point

The guilt after yelling at my kids, the endless to-do lists, the constant voice saying "not enough" finally pushed me to ask: What if there's another way to live? That question began my journey of healing. Louise Hay opened my heart. Wayne Dyer awakened my soul. NLP taught me I could rewire how I think, speak, and feel. Yoga and Kundalini helped me heal my body and reconnect with my spirit. Mental fitness showed me that peace and presence are choices I can make every day.

Why I Do This Work

Because I know the pain of feeling like you're never enough, no matter how much you do, of smiling on the outside while quietly aching inside, of losing touch with your dreams "because everyone else comes first." And I also know the freedom of coming home to yourself, of living with clarity, compassion, and joy.

The Woman I Am Today

Menopause became one of my greatest teachers, guiding me to shed old layers and light my fire in new ways. Today, I stand rooted in my truth: strong, joyful, and radiant. I help women reconnect with themselves, release what is heavy, and rise into the wise, powerful version of themselves they were always meant to be.
